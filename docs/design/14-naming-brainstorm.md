# Naming Brainstorm: Collective Intelligence and Coordination

This document explores potential names for the project that evoke the same
ideas as "agents" or "swarm" (collective intelligence, coordination, and
agility) while ensuring they are available as Unix utilities and lack major
AI-space conflicts.

## Candidates

### The "Collective Motion" Group

_These names directly reference the emergent behavior of groups._

- **Murmur** (from _Murmuration_): Evokes the complex, fluid coordination of
  starlings. It suggests a system that is more than the sum of its parts.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; very clean for a CLI (`murmur run`).
- **Skein**: Refers to a flock of geese in flight or a length of thread. It
  evokes both "formation-based" movement and the "threading" of tasks.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; unique and short.
- **Shoal**: A group of fish swimming together. It suggests a fluid, agile,
  and highly collaborative environment.
  - **Status:** Primarily legacy Java/scientific tools; no current mainstream
    CLI conflict.
- **Covey**: A small flock of birds (usually quail). It evokes a tight-knit,
  focused, and efficient group of agents.
  - **Status:** Completely clear in Homebrew and common AI naming.

### The "Action & Assembly" Group

_These names focus on the gathering and deployment of capabilities._

- **Muster**: To assemble or collect troops/agents for a specific task. It is
  a strong, action-oriented command.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; feels like a natural CLI verb
    (`muster deploy`).
- **Skulk**: A collective noun for foxes. It evokes "shrewd" intelligence,
  stealth, and coordinated cleverness.
  - **Status:** Clear in Homebrew; distinctive and "tech-noir."
- **Dray**: A squirrel’s nest or a group of squirrels. It suggests
  organization, caching, and industrious gathering.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; very short and easy to type.
- **Husk**: A collective noun for hares. It evokes speed and a "protective" or
  "contained" execution environment.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict.

### The "Intelligence & Precision" Group

_These names focus on the "agentic" qualities of the system._

- **Glint**: A small flash of light. It suggests precision, speed, and a
  "spark" of intelligence.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; extremely sharp and modern feel.
- **Wry**: Meaning clever, dryly humorous, or "twisted." It evokes a smart,
  adaptive system that handles edge cases with wit.
  - **Status:** Clear in Homebrew; very short (3 letters).
- **Spry**: Meaning nimble, active, and energetic. It suggests a lightweight,
  high-performance agent framework.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; evokes agility.
- **Verve**: Meaning vigor and spirit. It suggests a system with high
  throughput and "alive" interactions.
  - **Status:** No Homebrew conflict; sophisticated "vibe."
